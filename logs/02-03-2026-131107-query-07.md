# Research Report: Current State of Press Freedom and Journalism Safety Worldwide

**Date:** March 2, 2026 | **Sources consulted:** 7

## Executive Summary

Press freedom has reached an unprecedented crisis point. The 2025 RSF World Press Freedom Index classifies the global situation as "difficult" for the first time in its history, with over half the world's population (4.25 billion people in 42 countries) living in "very serious" press freedom situations. A record 129 journalists were killed in 2025—more than in any previous year in three decades of CPJ recordkeeping—with Israel responsible for two-thirds of those deaths. As of December 2025, 503 journalists were detained in 47 countries, with China (121), Russia (48), and Myanmar (47) as the top jailers. UNESCO reports a historic 10% decline in global freedom of expression between 2012 and 2024, driven by self-censorship, digital surveillance, and the weaponization of legal frameworks.

## Key Findings

### Record Violence Against Journalists in 2025
The year 2025 surpassed 2024's already-grim record. CPJ documented 129 journalists killed—the highest in three decades. The IFJ recorded 128 deaths including 10 women. The Middle East and Arab World accounted for 58% of all deaths, with 56 journalists killed in Palestine alone, 13 in Yemen, and 8 in Ukraine. Israel was responsible for two-thirds of all press killings worldwide. Africa accounted for 18 deaths, Asia Pacific for 15, the Americas for 11, and Europe for 10. Additionally, 135 journalists are currently missing in 37 countries and 20 are held hostage.

### Mass Imprisonment and Political Repression
As of December 1, 2025, 503 journalists were detained in 47 countries—significantly higher than the 361 reported in late 2024. China remains the world's largest jailer of journalists (121), now followed by Russia (48) and Myanmar (47). The RSF Index found that 112 out of assessed countries saw their overall press freedom scores decline. The average score fell below 55 points, placing the global state in the "difficult situation" category for the first time. Eastern Europe and Central Asia experienced the steepest regional decline.

### Converging Digital and Economic Threats
Journalists face multiple intersecting threats beyond physical violence. Digital surveillance using tools like Pegasus, Predator, and Paragon's Graphite continues to target journalists globally, with investigations in late 2025 documenting cases from Mexico to India. Economic fragility has emerged as a leading threat: the RSF economic indicator is at an unprecedented low, with local news in the U.S. especially affected, creating "vast regions" of news deserts. The U.S. has fallen to 57th in the index, classified as a "problematic situation." Governments are increasingly using legal and regulatory frameworks to control media, broadening state oversight through digital laws, cybersecurity rules, and licensing regulations.

## Areas of Uncertainty

- Whether the EU's anti-SLAPP legislation will meaningfully reduce legal harassment of journalists across member states.
- The long-term impact of newsroom economic collapse on the quality and diversity of global journalism.
- How emerging AI-generated disinformation will compound existing threats to press credibility and journalist safety.
- Whether international accountability mechanisms will address the unprecedented scale of journalist killings in conflict zones.
